Biography

Yongqiang Lyu is currently an associate professor at National Research Center for Information Science ant Technology of Tsinghua University. He was a visiting professor of University of Maryland at College Part from August 201   7 to July 2018.

His major research interests focus on processor security, including processor vulnerability mining, processor security verification and design.

As a major contributor, he discovered a vital vulnerability widely existing in Intel and ARM processors, named as VoltJockey (CVE-2019-11157), through which the attackers can attack the Intel SGX and ARM TrustZone. Intel officailly approves and awards the finding. Later, its variant on Nvidia GPUs, named as “Lightning”, was discovered and reported to Nvidia, which does not need the adversarial samples to hack the deep neural network outputs. Most recently, his group also discovered another variant of Spectre, named as “MDPhantom”, which can attack kernel secrets, and a new kind of side channel, named as “PMU side channel”, which can spy system secrets by using PMU counters.

He was awarded the AsianHost 2019 Best Paper, and the CHI 2015 Honorable Mention Award. Being one of the major contributors, he was also awarded several important national awards, including the first prize of CCF Natural Science Award (2020, 2/4), the first prize of Technology Progress Award of Chinese Institute of Electronics (2019, 8/15), the second prize of National Science and Technology Progress Award (2015, 4/10), etc.
